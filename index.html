Research Overview

My research focuses on computational optics, holography and computational displays. I'm particularly interested in developing next-generation novel 3D displays and near-eye displays, which combine advances in optical and computational techniques. My recent research includes hologram optimization, holographic projection and holographic near-eye displays.

Hologram Optimization Towards Photorealistic Holographic Displays
Holographic displays is inherent with speckles and artifacts due to the random walks of coherent light, which severely degrades the visual perception in holographic displays. Advanced optimization method can be combined with in-depth optical knowledge to achieve a speckle-free, artifacts-free photorealistic holographic displays.
Light-efficient and Compact Computational Holographic Projection
Holographic Projection is the desirable technique of image formation due to its high light-efficiency, compact configuration and potential of multi-depth image projection. Adavanced computational and optical techniques help to achieve a high-quality holographic projection with a simple, cheap and robust system.
Holographic Near-eye Displays for Virtual and Augmented Reality
Three-dimensional (3D) displays with continuous depth sensation has a profound impact on virtual and augmented reality applications. Computer holography can enable a high-spatial-angular-resolution 3D displays by numerical simulation of light diffraction and inteference. Adavanced computational and optical techniques help to achieve a real-time photorealistic holographic near-eye displays for VR/AR.
